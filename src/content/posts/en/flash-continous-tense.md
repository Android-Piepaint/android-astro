# Part 1

What are you gazing at,</br>
Upon that pitch-black screen?</br>
Far away on the hills,</br>
The scent of pine comes through.</br>
</br>

Today just like any other days, that passes in pain and gloom,</br>
Time just drifts along,</br>
Slipping right through the room.</br>
In my own memory, this world always stay the same,</br>
A "Walled Garden" built, of proprietary shame.</br>
</br>

We run together now, toward to the freedom where we seek,</br>
Proprietary software that we waved final "Goodbye" and we will never meet.</br>
Can't find an FOSS alternative, so from family I'm detached,</br>
Just remember well, the first time that the "Walled Garden" I unlatched.</br>
</br>
Long since, I’ve been used to, the golden cage,</br>
Not even grasping, what "freedom" really means.</br>

# Part 2

Feeling unconsciously, just wandering alone on the roof,</br>
Chasing the future needing the proof,</br>
to build a free and open phone.</br>

Finally, there's a day, my dream comes true,</br>
Bootloader unlock is my need.</br>
Typing command lines ,then power light shines,</br>
Saying "Walled Garden," goodbye!</br>

Logs start to scroll, acrossing all the blank screen,</br>
Shaking my heart, there is a sight that I never seen.</br>
</br>

Just 'cause "proprietary" broke it, the phone is near its end,</br>
The teen who loved to flash, has now grown up again.</br>
Even if it bricks, or Crashdump, it's fine, flashing's still continuous tense,</br>
If I’m feeling sad, just switch a new device,</br>
And start it all from scratch.</br>
</br>
Beyond the walls, there is, a freedom true,</br>
Found my own place, a place to call my own.</br>
</br>

# Bridge

Phone comes back home, time to starts again,</br>
"Unlock" conditions, seem tougher now than then.</br>
Masters of code, with ease I will thrive,</br>
Laughing in Linux, feeling alive!</br>

# Ending

We run together now, toward to the freedom where we seek,</br>
Proprietary software that we waved final "Goodbye" and we will never meet.</br>
Not caring all the technical details, but I remember that day,</br>
The courage to change, the brave practice that paved the way.</br>
</br>
Relying on code, that's closed to the eye,</br>
Until I knew, how to soar in the sky.</br>
Privacy now, is mine to command,</br>
Come on, let’s laugh out loud, again.</br>
